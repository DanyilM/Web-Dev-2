About the Benz Patent-Motorwagen

The Benz Patent-Motorwagen, created in 1885, was the world's first gasoline-powered automobile.

One of the remarkable aspects of the Motorwagen was its engineering ingenuity, offering a paradigm shift in personal transportation.

In 1888, Bertha Benz embarked on a historic journey to prove the vehicle's reliability. This journey not only showcased the potential of the Motorwagen but also became one of the first public relations stunts in history.

The Motorwagen paved the way for further innovations, leading to mass production techniques that made automobiles essential in modern life.

Key Features of the Benz Patent-Motorwagen

Single-Cylinder Internal Combustion Engine: Powered by a 0.75-horsepower engine using gasoline.
Lightweight Chassis Design: Made of tubular steel for durability and ease of maneuverability.
Innovative Steering Mechanism: A tiller guided its front wheels.
Rubber Tires: Reduced friction and enhanced ride comfort.
Manual Drive Chain: Transmitted power to rear wheels via a manual chain-drive system.
Carburetor for Fuel Efficiency: Allowed efficient fuel combustion, a precursor to modern fuel systems.
Cooling System: Water-cooled engine to prevent overheating.
Compact Fuel Tank: Efficiently designed and located at the rear.
